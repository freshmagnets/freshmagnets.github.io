Photo Magnet Size Guide: Square vs Rectangle vs Round

Three shapes, three jobs. Here is how to pick the right photo magnet size for your photo and the moment you want to keep.

Updated June 20264 min read
The short version
  • Square (2.5″): the all-rounder and best seller, great for almost any photo.
  • Rectangle (2″×3″): classic photo shape, best for upright portraits.
  • Round (2.25″): playful and eye-catching, best for faces and party favors.

All three Fresh Magnets sizes use the same premium build: full-color print, a smooth glossy finish, and a strong magnetic backing, pressed to order in the USA. The shape you choose is really about your photo and how you want it displayed. Let's break it down.

Square: 2.5″ × 2.5″

The square is our most popular size, and it earns it. It's big enough to show a face beautifully but compact enough that a pack of them clusters into a tidy gallery on the fridge door. Square framing also forgives a lot, group shots, close-ups, food, landscapes and Instagram-style photos all sit comfortably inside it.

If you only order one shape, make it the square. Start with custom photo magnets in a 6- or 12-pack and build from there.

Rectangle: 2″ × 3″

The rectangle matches the proportions of a traditional photo print, so it feels familiar the moment it lands on the fridge. It's the size people reach for when a moment deserves to be framed, not just remembered: upright portraits, couples, pets with room around them, and vertical travel shots all shine here.

For people-first photos, the fridge photo magnets in this rectangle size are the easy pick.

Round: 2.25″

The round cut is the wildcard. A circle draws the eye and crops in tight on smiles, so it stands out from every square and rectangle around it. It's a hit as a party favor and save-the-date because it looks playful and feels a little special. Tight head-and-shoulders shots work best, busy backgrounds get lost in the crop.

Reach for personalized photo magnets when you want something fun that guests will actually keep.

Still not sure? Mix them

You don't have to commit to one. Mixing sizes across a single order makes a layered, collected-over-time look on the fridge, a few rectangles for portraits, squares for the group shots, and a round or two for fun. Every shape comes in packs of 6, 9, 12 and 15, so it's easy to build a set.
