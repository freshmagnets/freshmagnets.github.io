Wedding Photo Magnets: Favors & Save-the-Dates Guests Keep

Most wedding favors end up in a drawer. A photo magnet ends up on the fridge, seen every day, for years. Here's how to do them well.

Updated June 20265 min read

There's a simple test for a good wedding favor: a month later, does the guest still have it? Candles burn down, koozies disappear, and little boxed treats get eaten before the drive home. A photo magnet passes the test. It goes straight onto the fridge and earns a permanent spot next to the grocery list and the kids' drawings.

That's why couples keep choosing wedding photo magnets for both save-the-dates and reception favors. They're personal, genuinely useful, and inexpensive per guest.

Save-the-dates that don't get recycled

A paper save-the-date is lucky to survive the week. A magnet version sticks to the fridge with your date front and center until the big day arrives, a built-in countdown your guests can't misplace. Use a relaxed engagement photo, keep the text short (names + the date), and you've got a save-the-date that does its one job perfectly.

The round shape stands out from everything else on the fridge, which is exactly what you want for a date you need people to remember. Browse the personalized photo magnets for that look.

Reception favors guests actually take home

Lay a basket of magnets by the exit or set one at each place setting with a small "thank you" tag. Because they're flat and light, they travel home easily, no fragile glass, nothing to spill. Weeks later, your photo is still on the fridge as a small daily reminder of a great night.

Choosing the photo

  • Faces over scenery. The magnet is small, so a tight shot of the two of you reads far better than a wide venue photo.
  • Bright and high-resolution. Send the largest version of the file you have. Crisp, well-lit photos print sharpest.
  • Mind the crop. For round magnets, keep faces centered and avoid important details near the edges.

Which size for weddings?

There's no wrong answer, but here's the quick steer:

  • Square (2.5″): the versatile favorite, flatters almost any engagement or couple photo. Shop custom photo magnets.
  • Rectangle (2″×3″): classic and formal, ideal for an upright portrait. Shop fridge photo magnets.
  • Round (2.25″): playful and eye-catching, great for save-the-dates and favors.

Not sure? Our photo magnet size guide compares all three side by side.

How many should you order?

A good rule of thumb is one magnet per household or per guest, plus around 10% extra for spares, keepsakes and the inevitable "can I have one too?" Packs come in 6, 9, 12 and 15, and you can stack packs to hit your headcount. For larger guest lists, bulk photo magnets are the most economical route.

Frequently asked questions

Are photo magnets good wedding favors?
Yes, they're useful, personal and low-cost per guest, and they actually get kept because they live on the fridge instead of in a drawer.
How many favor magnets should I order?
Plan for about one per guest or household, plus ~10% extra. Combine packs of 6, 9, 12 or 15 to reach your number, or use bulk photo magnets for big counts.
Can I use the same design for save-the-dates and favors?
Absolutely. Many couples reuse the engagement photo for the save-the-date and a wedding-day photo for the favor, keeping a consistent look across both.
